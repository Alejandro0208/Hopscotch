Final Project Finger Hopscotch




About the Piece


The theme of the piece is childhood. We hoped to recreate a unique version of the playful game that we often played
as children. The result was a finger-sized hopscotch game, where it lights up as the user's fingers press buttons
corresponding to the hopscotch squares.


Because our buttons were relatively small, it was difficult to secure them while keeping them connected to the
wired to the breadboard. We solved this problem by using the wooden circles left over from laser cutting our
enclosure to act as tops for the buttons. To connect the buttons and wires, we soldered them. This proved to be
another issue, since we originally soldered the buttons in a way that didnâ€™t allow for the current to switch on and
off. We solved them by resoldering the wires. Making and assembling the box was both a difficult and entertaining
experience. The combination of laser cutting and using saws was something we had never done before.


We decided to have lights exposed as opposed to keeping them within the enclosure in order to get as much color
as possible to stay true the vibrancy and playfulness of hopscotch. We also made it so the buttons are surrounded
by little laser cut squares to emulate the squares in hopscotch.

Setup

Easily the most difficult part of recreating the projecting is actually building the enclosure. We recommend using
